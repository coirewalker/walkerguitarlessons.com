Lessons

My music degree is from Musicians Institute (MI) in Los Angeles, CA. While attending MI, I had the honor of studying under world-class guitarists like Paul Gilbert, Alan Holdsworth, Alan Hinds, Ross Bolton, Dan Gilbert, Al Bonhomme, David Oakes, Dale Turner, and Travis Carlton.

Lessons are given in Port Angeles at Peninsula Music and on Zoom worldwide. I am very organized and work to develop practical structures and approaches that provide results. I use Google Drive for student collaboration, practice routines, notation/tabs, and demonstration videos I create as study guides. Having all lesson content available provides a robust study framework that is highly organized and accessible from anywhere. Although I go into each lesson with an informal plan, student feedback determines how we move forward. Growth is dynamic, and so is the approach. Effective practice structures deliver results at home. Having my students understand the connection between technical and theoretical aspects in a way that allows a healthy, self-examined, and adventurous relationship with music is what I strive to accomplish.

I am happy to meet you for a phone or video chat to answer questions and see if we are a good fit. Weekly lessons are ideal, but we can meet whenever you are available.

What to expect as a beginner

If you are a beginning player, my primary objective is to provide you with the structure required, familiarize you with the instrument, and nurture your curiosity. You will learn playing fundamentals and how to approach practice with a positive mindset. During our first session, we will get to know each other, assess your goals, break ground on your practice routine, and give you something simple to start playing on your guitar. It's fun, I promise! My goal is to have you leave our first session with a sense that learning to play guitar is not only possible but an enriching challenge.

What to expect as an experienced player

If you have some experience with guitar, what we decide to work on together depends on your goals. Where are you stuck? Do you need help with your rhythm? Are YouTube videos creating more questions than answers? Do you want a more comprehensive understanding of music theory, ear training, or to write better songs? Maybe you can't put your finger on the issue, which is quite common. Whatever your reasons, I can teach you the tools and techniques to get unstuck. Perspective is everything.

Rates

Although I can afford to charge top rates for my lessons, I want to reach the people who need music most. Often, this is the person working three jobs trying to make rent or the kid in the punk band with big dreams. I was that kid, and I had no one to teach me (I wish I had). I believe that learning music should be accessible to everyone. For this reason, rates are on a sliding scale.

If you can afford the upper rate, please pay it. If you can't afford that, so long as you are willing to work hard, I am happy to work with you at an affordable rate. Your success is most important to me. This decision is my way of giving back and making the world more equitable.

Sliding Scale:

60min: $45-85
45min: $35-65
30min: $25-45

I accept Venmo, PayPal, and ApplePay for payment. If you are interested in a lesson, please review my lesson policy.
