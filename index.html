About Me

I have been playing guitar for twenty-one years. I have been teaching people how to play guitar for twelve years. I earned my music degree in 2005 from Musicians Institute in Los Angeles. I like to balance the technical with the musical in order to build people into healthy, creative, thinking musicians over the longterm.

You can see me teach on my YouTube channel here.

I also write and perform music around New York City. You can find it here.

Mostly I just enjoy sharing how cool I think music is, while helping my students get where they want to be. The longterm process of teaching is what it's all about for me. I teach all ages, from children to adults, to older adults (it's never too late!). The youngest student I ever taught was 4 years old--the oldest was 76.

I teach all styles of music, except advanced jazz and classical guitar (these are their own discipline). Some styles I enjoy are folk, rock, metal, punk, reggae, funk, RNB, indie, post rock, and hip hop. Some of the lesson topics we might cover are music theory, reading, ear training, strumming and picking techniques, learning to sing and play simultaniously, songwriting approaches, soloing, learning songs you love or even working on your own original material. What's most important is that my students are happy as they grow into the musicians they want to be.
